Introduction

Happiness is subjective in many ways. You have the option to influence and control your attitude, and you can choose how you view the obstacles in your life. Are they a hinderance, or a blessing in disguise? Are you going to learn from the obstacle you were faced with, or are you going to dwell on it? Many times, that decision is up to you to make. Just as much as outlook influences our daily levels of general happiness, though, so does the way we live our average life. The physical and mental aspects of any person's being have to be harmoniously balanced in order for any true "peace." One does not matter more than the other. That being said, it is sometimes easier to take care of the physical aspect than it is the mental one. Our physical surroundings and conduct is what affects our mental health the most-- they both rely on one another. It's also important to note that no person is alike, and that subsequently neither is any path. What makes someone else happy may not make you happy (and vice versa). Someone may achieve something faster than you did, but that does not essentially mean that they worked harder for it or that you didn't deserve what you thought would make you happy. The universe has a way of planning things out for you-- often times, the missed opportunity is just an opening door towards a better one.

Question

"Do I live in the present moment?" In a way, we are not taught to accept our thoughts but to fight against them. The best way to accept thoughts is through surrender. When you give up and accept what is happening to you in the present moment, it is then that great changes begin to occur in your life, however, if you resist, you will be in a constant loop of emptiness in which you will unconsciously feel yourself a and again. When we continually think about the past, without knowing it, we dive into a sea of ​​worries and constantly relive those situations in our mind in a random way, that is why our thoughts travel from here to there through our mind without a fixed course, generating In your body the feeling of discomfort. However, when we constantly think about the future, we begin to notice anxiety and some stress due to the simple fact of not being able to control what will happen. Now, what happens if we think about the present? Although perhaps the most appropriate question is what if instead of thinking about the present moment, we simply live it? And you will ask yourself and how do you live in the present moment while still thinking? Well, through the sensations. Feel free to feel what you are feeling, pay attention to those sensations that your body is reproducing at this moment, what do you feel? Keeping our mind in the present helps us feel free, since we are consciously accepting what is happening to us at this time.
